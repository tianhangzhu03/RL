Why XLF for this comparison?

XLF is used as the mainline asset because it is liquid, diversified at the sector level, and still contains meaningful drawdown-recovery cycles. This gives a clearer risk-return testbed than a single-name momentum stock.

Practical role: this is not a signal-generation contest; it is a policy-selection problem under constraints. The gate decides whether utility gains are acceptable once downside risk is considered.

What each algorithm contributes

Q-learning

Off-policy baseline. Useful to test whether aggressive utility improvement appears, and whether risk checks reject that behavior.

SG-Sarsa

On-policy linear approximation. Useful for testing sensitivity to learning rate, exploration schedule, and episode budget.

n-step Sarsa

Multi-step credit assignment. Useful for balancing utility gain and risk drift under the synchronized gate.

Candidate-level outcomes on XLF (from headline table)

  • Comparison purpose: isolate algorithm behavior under identical environment and gate.
  • Actual use: choose deployable policy candidates, not just the highest unconstrained return.
  • Decision criterion: promotion under risk constraints is stronger than raw uplift.

Interpretation guide
  • Higher target variance can produce faster utility uplift but unstable risk behavior.
